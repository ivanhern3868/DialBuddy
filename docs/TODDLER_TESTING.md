# Toddler Usability Testing Protocol

## Purpose
This document outlines the structured approach for testing DialBuddy with actual 3-4 year old children. The primary goal is to ensure the app is usable, engaging, and **emotionally safe** (especially the emergency module).

**Critical Business Requirement:** An app designed for toddlers MUST be tested with toddlers. Adult testing alone is insufficient to validate usability and emotional safety.

**Ethical Considerations:**
- Testing is strictly observational - we are testing the APP, not the child
- No child performance data is recorded or shared
- Parents must be present and can stop testing at any time
- Children can quit whenever they want - never pressured to continue
- **Anonymous observation only** - no photos, videos, names, or identifying information collected (COPPA compliance)

---

## Testing Schedule & Milestones

### Test Session #1: Basic Dialer UX
- **When:** End of Phase 1 (Week 3)
- **What's Being Tested:** Free Dial mode with DTMF tones and visual feedback
- **Duration:** 5-10 minutes per child
- **Minimum Sample:** 5 children (ages 3-4)

### Test Session #2: Guided Practice
- **When:** End of Phase 2 (Week 5)
- **What's Being Tested:** Practice mode with pulsing hints and celebrations
- **Duration:** 10-15 minutes per child
- **Minimum Sample:** 5 children

### Test Session #3: Emergency Scenario Recognition (CRITICAL)
- **When:** End of Phase 3A (Week 7)
- **What's Being Tested:** YES/NO scenario cards, Buddy mascot tone, dispatcher simulation
- **Duration:** 15-20 minutes per child
- **Minimum Sample:** 5 children
- **SAFETY GATE:** If ANY child shows signs of fear/distress, STOP and redesign before proceeding

### Test Session #4: Full Emergency Module
- **When:** End of Phase 3B (Week 9)
- **What's Being Tested:** Complete emergency flow with after-call actions
- **Duration:** 20 minutes per child
- **Minimum Sample:** 5 children

### Test Session #5: Game Modes
- **When:** End of Phase 4 (Week 11)
- **What's Being Tested:** Simon Says, Who's Calling?, engagement level
- **Duration:** 15 minutes per child
- **Minimum Sample:** 5 children

### Test Session #6: Localization
- **When:** End of Phase 5 (Week 13)
- **What's Being Tested:** Spanish and Portuguese TTS, translations
- **Duration:** 10 minutes per child
- **Minimum Sample:** 3 Spanish-speaking children + 3 Portuguese-speaking children

## Test Session Structure

### Pre-Session Setup (5 minutes before child arrives)
1. Device fully charged, volume at 70%
2. App reset to fresh state (clear any test data)
3. Parent contact added to test Practice Mode (use test phone number)
4. Observation sheet printed and ready
5. Parent consent obtained (verbal is sufficient; documented by tester initials)

### Session Flow

**1. Welcome & Warm-Up (2 minutes)**
- Introduce yourself to child and parent
- Explain to child: "We made a fun phone game and want to see if you like it!"
- Show child the device, let them hold it
- Emphasize: "There are no wrong answers - you can't make a mistake!"

**2. Guided Exploration (varies by test)**
- Hand device to child
- Provide minimal instruction: "What do you see?" or "What do you want to try?"
- Allow child to explore freely for 1-2 minutes
- Observe: Do they tap buttons? Do they react to sounds? Do they understand icons?

**3. Specific Task Testing (varies by test)**
- See "Phase-Specific Testing Protocols" below
- Tester may provide hints ONLY if child is stuck for >30 seconds
- Never force a child to complete a task - note abandonment as data

**4. Cool-Down & Debrief (2 minutes)**
- Ask child: "Did you have fun?" (yes/no only - don't pressure for details)
- Ask child: "Was anything scary or not fun?" (open-ended, watch body language)
- Thank child with a sticker or small reward (non-food)

**5. Parent Interview (5 minutes, child can play freely)**
- "Would you feel comfortable letting [child] use this alone?"
- "Did you notice any confusion or frustration?"
- "Would you trust this app to teach emergency skills?"
- "Is there anything that concerned you?"

## Phase-Specific Testing Protocols

### Phase 1: Basic Dialer UX

**Tasks:**
1. "Can you press some numbers?"
2. "What sound does it make?"
3. "Can you press the green button?"

**Success Criteria:**
- 4/5 children successfully tap number buttons
- 4/5 children react to DTMF sounds (turn to look, smile, etc.)
- 3/5 children understand green button means "call"

**Key Observations:**
- Are 80×80px buttons large enough?
- Do children notice the number appearing on screen?
- Do they understand cause-and-effect (tap → sound)?

---

### Phase 2: Guided Practice

**Tasks:**
1. "See the number that's glowing? Can you press it?"
2. Complete a 3-digit practice sequence (beginner mode)
3. Experience the celebration animation

**Success Criteria:**
- 4/5 children follow the pulsing hint without verbal instruction
- 3/5 children complete the sequence independently
- 5/5 children smile/react positively to celebration

**Key Observations:**
- Is the hint obvious enough?
- Do they understand they're "dialing" a person (concept comprehension)?
- Is the celebration rewarding without being overstimulating?

---

### Phase 3A: Emergency Scenario Recognition (CRITICAL TEST)

**Setup:**
- Ensure parent is present and briefed: "We're testing emergency scenarios. Please watch your child's reaction closely."
- Have parent comfort items nearby (favorite toy, snack)

**Tasks:**
1. Show 3 YES scenarios (fire, someone hurt, stranger danger)
2. Show 3 NO scenarios (bored, sibling fight, scraped knee)
3. Listen to Buddy explain "when to call [emergency number]"
4. Navigate one simple dispatcher conversation

**Success Criteria:**
- 0/5 children show fear or distress (HARD REQUIREMENT)
- 3/5 children answer YES/NO correctly on at least 4/6 scenarios
- 4/5 children can tap response options in dispatcher sim
- 5/5 parents feel comfortable with the tone

**SAFETY PROTOCOL:**
If a child shows fear:
1. Stop immediately
2. Switch to a fun activity (Free Dial mode or similar)
3. Document exactly what triggered fear (scenario? Buddy's voice? Dispatcher?)
4. Interview parent about child's reaction
5. Do NOT test emergency content with other children until redesign is complete

**Key Observations:**
- Is Buddy's tone friendly and reassuring?
- Are scenario illustrations clear but not graphic?
- Do children understand the YES/NO concept?
- Does the "secret agent mission" framing work for silent call lesson?
- Is the dispatcher simulation realistic without being scary?

**Red Flag Scenarios to Watch:**
- Fire scenario (flames might frighten)
- "Someone won't wake up" (concept of unconsciousness)
- Stranger danger (might trigger existing fears)
- Hiding/staying quiet (might feel scary, not empowering)

---

### Phase 3B: Full Emergency Module

**Tasks:**
1. Complete one scenario card → dial emergency number → dispatcher sim → after-call actions
2. Practice "don't hang up" concept
3. Navigate fire escape after-call checklist

**Success Criteria:**
- 0/5 children show fear (continued requirement)
- 3/5 children can navigate full flow with minimal hints
- 4/5 children understand "don't hang up" (repeat the phrase when asked)
- 3/5 children can describe at least one after-call action when prompted

**Key Observations:**
- Is the flow too long (attention span)?
- Do children understand "go to your meeting spot"?
- Is "unlock the door" a safe instruction to teach? (depends on home setup)
- Do they internalize "firefighters are here to help" (friendly, not scary)?

---

### Phase 4: Game Modes

**Tasks:**
1. Play one round of Simon Says
2. Attempt Who's Calling? for a known contact

**Success Criteria:**
- 3/5 children voluntarily play Simon Says twice (engagement)
- No frustration when they make mistakes (gentle feedback)
- 2/5 children can recall at least 3 digits of a practiced number

**Key Observations:**
- Is Simon Says fun or frustrating?
- Is Who's Calling? too hard (if so, increase mastery unlock threshold)?

---

### Phase 5: Localization

**Tasks:**
1. Test TTS pronunciation in Spanish or Portuguese
2. Show translated scenario cards

**Success Criteria:**
- Parents confirm TTS is understandable (accent may be imperfect but comprehensible)
- Children react appropriately to voice prompts in their language
- Translations are culturally appropriate (parent confirmation)

**Key Observations:**
- Any awkward phrasings that need professional translator review?
- Are emergency numbers correct for parent's home country?

## Data Analysis & Reporting

After each test session:

1. **Immediate Debrief (same day):**
   - Review observation sheets
   - Identify critical issues (especially emotional safety red flags)
   - Decide: Proceed to next phase OR redesign?

2. **Aggregate Analysis (after all children in a phase):**
   - Calculate task completion rates
   - Identify common confusion points
   - Extract themes from open observations

3. **Design Iteration:**
   - Prioritize fixes based on severity:
     - **P0 (Blocker):** Emotional safety issues, parent concerns about trust
     - **P1 (Critical):** >50% of children can't complete core tasks
     - **P2 (Important):** Confusion points, usability friction
     - **P3 (Nice to have):** Polish improvements

4. **Documentation:**
   - Summary report for each phase
   - Store observation sheets in secure location (physical or encrypted digital)
   - Destroy any identifying information after summary is created

## Ethical & Legal Compliance

### COPPA Compliance
- **No personal information collected:** We observe behavior only, never record names, birthdates, or identifiable details
- **No video/photo:** Strictly prohibited during testing
- **Parent consent:** Required before each session (verbal consent is sufficient; tester initials on observation sheet)
- **Right to withdraw:** Child or parent can stop at any time without explanation

### Data Security
- Observation sheets stored in locked file or password-protected folder
- Only aggregate, anonymized data used in reports
- Individual observation sheets destroyed 30 days after phase completion

### Compensation (Optional)
- Small sticker or non-food reward for child participation
- No monetary compensation (avoids coercion)
- Parents may receive $10 gift card for time (if budget allows)

## Emergency Contact Information

If a child becomes distressed during testing:

1. **Immediate response:** Comfort child, stop test, return to parent
